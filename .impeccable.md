# Resumify — Design Context

## Users

People building a resume — most often technical (engineers, designers, PMs, data folks) but the audience is anyone who values a deliberate, well-crafted tool. Resume editing is **infrequent and high-stakes**: people use it for an hour or two every couple of years and want it to feel like time well spent. The interface itself is part of the deliverable's quality signal.

The novel mechanic — **assembling a resume on a node canvas** instead of filling a form — is the product. It rewards people who like Figma, n8n, Logic Pro, Notion-with-databases, music DAWs.

## Brand Personality

The product has license to feel **opinionated and confident**. It doesn't have to be the safe, beige choice. Three words: **deliberate, characterful, generous**. Generous spacing, generous typography, generous animations — but never wasteful.

Tone-of-voice across all variants:
- Lower-case lockup is fine
- Mono numerals + serial IDs everywhere we can earn them
- Status microcopy in present-tense, no exclamation points
- Empty states should *teach the mechanic*, not say "nothing here"

## Aesthetic Direction

The base app is "engineering tool dressed in editorial type" — dark, indigo, dashed motifs. Variants must each commit to a **distinct identity** as if they were separate products in the same family. No safe middle-ground variants — each one pushes into its own corner.

### V1 — Atelier (warm, paper, letterpress)

Light cream paper, sepia ink, antique gold + brick accents. The resume-as-art-object. Penguin Classics meets a small letterpress shop.
- Display: **Vollkorn** (warm transitional serif)
- Body: **Karla** (humanist sans with quirks)
- Mono: **JetBrains Mono** small caps for marginalia
- Theme: light, warm, textured

### V2 — Terminal (mono, terminal, status-dense)

Power-user terminal aesthetic. Mono everywhere, status-bar density, phosphor green on void, ASCII box-drawing as decoration.
- Type: **JetBrains Mono** (display + body); small labels in **Archivo Narrow**
- Theme: dark, near-black with green tint

### V3 — Riso (print studio, zine, two-color overlap)

Risograph-printed art zine. Hot pink + royal blue ink overlapping with deliberate misregistration, halftone textures, big bold display type.
- Display: **Bricolage Grotesque** (variable, expressive width/weight)
- Body: **Hanken Grotesk**
- Theme: light cream paper with print colors

## Design Principles

1. **Each surface earns its character.** No element is generic — sidebar, top bar, node cards, edges, preview paper all signal the variant's identity.
2. **One brand color, used sparingly.** Solid accents > gradients. Where two colors are needed (Riso), they're print-system colors with deliberate roles.
3. **Mono labels for the engineering layer; display type for the editorial layer.** Numbers, IDs, edge labels, kbd hints belong in mono. Headings and human content belong in the display face.
4. **Dashed motifs as a through-line in the base app.** Variants may keep, transform, or replace this — but the choice must be intentional.
5. **No AI-tells.** No purple-on-dark gradients, no left-stripe alert cards, no gradient text, no glassmorphism for its own sake, no lonely cyan accents.
